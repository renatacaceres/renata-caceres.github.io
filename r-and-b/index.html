With everyone’s collaboration, we can maximize the chances of a successful and safe event for everyone, especially those of advanced age.

Travel Info

The Venue Address

Our wedding elebration is happening at The Venue CU, located at 51 East Main St, in downtown Champaign.

The Venue CU's buildings are National Historic Landmarks and important parts to the history of Champaign-Urbana. What today is called the Varsity Ballroom, where our event is taking place, was originally the Varsity Theater, opened in 1906. The Vriner's Confectionery, opened in 1898, today is the Main Street Lounge, adjacent to the Varsity Ballroom.

Phone Number: 217-419-7964

How to get to Champaign, IL

By plane:

  • The easiest way to arrive by flying is at the local CMI airport. This is also the most costly option and requires flying through ORD or DFW.
  • For lower prices and more options, fly into one of Chicago’s airports, ORD or MDW. Then rent a car and drive (ORD and MDW are about a 2h30m and 2h15m drive from Champaign, respectively, depending on traffic). If you don’t want to rent a car, you can reserve the Peoria Charter bus to Illinois Terminal in downtown Champaign (approximately 3-4h), or if you are a rail fan take a 30+ minute taxi or public transit ride to downtown Chicago and grab the Amtrak train to Champaign.
  • Other choices are flying into BMI in Bloomington, IL (approximately 1h drive), or IND in Indianapolis (approximately 2h drive).

By train:

  • Amtrak has two trains that stop in Champaign.

Where to Stay

The Hyatt Place (about $205/night including taxes & fees, via Expedia) is a high-quality hotel in downtown Champaign. The Inman is a historic hotel that was recently renovated; its management company is renting some rooms via Airbnb ($157). Both are within an easy 3-block walk of the venue and a variety of nice restaurants, cafes, and shops.

Many other hotels and AirBnBs are within a 5-10 minute drive. The Hampton Inn ($121) is just 1 mile away. The I-Hotel ($169) is an upscale hotel run by the university, but far enough away to avoid the congestion of the main campus. You can find these and others on your favorite travel site, or ask us if you have any questions.
